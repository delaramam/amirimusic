Persian Classical Music

Persian classical music is a timeless art form that reflects centuries of culture and spirituality. At its heart is the radif, a collection of melodic patterns that inspire both improvisation and composition. Its gentle rhythms and beautiful melodies create a connection between the performer and the audience.

Featured Image
Artist Photo

As a tombak player, my goal is to honor this rich tradition while incorporating my personal touch. I am inspired by the deep roots of Persian classical music and aim to push the boundaries of what the tombak can express. Through solo performances and collaborations, I strive to showcase the tombak as a powerful instrument that connects the timeless beauty of Persian music with modern interpretations. My hope is to inspire others to experience the emotions and beauty that Persian classical music offers.

Biography

Bio Photo

Delaram Amiri is a passionate tombak player dedicated to preserving and evolving the rich tradition of Persian classical music. With deep roots in the radif system and a commitment to authentic expression, Delaram brings both technical mastery and emotional depth to every performance.

Through collaborations with renowned musicians and solo performances, Delaram continues to explore the boundaries of Persian percussion while honoring its centuries-old traditions.

Faraz Ensemble

The Faraz Ensemble brings together traditional Persian instruments with contemporary arrangements, creating a unique sound that honors the past while embracing the present. Led by accomplished musicians, the ensemble features Ali Ghaemmaghami (tar), Behrooz Alavi (ney), Adrian Golay (cello), and Delaram Amiri (tombak).
